# Project Brief: 4050

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** November 28, 2025  
**Status:** Draft

---

## Executive Summary

4050 is an e-commerce platform selling homemade artisan products crafted from heritage apples and other produce grown on-site. The business transforms a long-standing tradition of gifting handmade apple products (applesauce, apple butter, jams, jellies) into a social enterprise that serves both local and online customers while donating profits to support communities in need through food banks, educational programs, and community centers.

---

## Problem Statement

**Current State:**
Many communities lack access to authentic, homemade artisan food products made from heritage ingredients. Additionally, local charitable organizations consistently need sustainable funding sources to support their missions.

**The Gap:**
- Consumers seeking high-quality, handcrafted food products often face limited options beyond mass-produced alternatives
- Small-scale producers struggle to reach customers beyond their immediate networks
- Charitable giving often feels disconnected from everyday purchasing decisions
- Heritage agricultural practices and heirloom varieties are underutilized

**Why Now:**
The proven quality of these products (successfully gifted for years) combined with growing consumer interest in artisan foods, sustainable agriculture, and purpose-driven brands creates an ideal opportunity to launch a business that generates both revenue and community impact.

---

## Proposed Solution

4050 is an online store that makes heritage apple products accessible to customers nationwide while maintaining the authenticity and quality of homemade craftsmanship.

**Core Concept:**
A simple, beautiful e-commerce website where customers can purchase artisan apple products with the knowledge that their purchase directly supports local communities.

**Key Differentiators:**
- **Heritage Ingredients:** Products made from heritage apples grown on-site
- **Proven Quality:** Years of recipe refinement through gifting
- **Made with Love:** Authentic homemade products, not mass-produced
- **Social Impact:** Transparent profit-sharing with community organizations
- **Flexibility:** Both shipping and local pickup options

**Why This Will Succeed:**
The combination of authentic product quality, meaningful social impact, and the growing market for artisan foods creates a compelling value proposition that resonates with conscious consumers.

**Goals:**

## Goals & Success Metrics

### Business Objectives

- **Launch MVP within 3-4 months** with core product catalog and e-commerce functionality
- **Generate first 50 orders within 60 days of launch** to validate market fit
- **Achieve $10,000 in revenue within first 6 months** to establish business viability
- **Donate minimum $1,000 to community organizations within first year** to demonstrate social impact
- **Maintain 4.5+ star average customer rating** to ensure product quality and customer satisfaction

### User Success Metrics

- **Purchase completion rate >60%** (users who add to cart complete purchase)
- **Repeat customer rate >25% within 3 months** (customers return for additional purchases)
- **Average order value >$40** (customers find value in bundling products)
- **Local pickup adoption >30%** of orders from pickup-area customers
- **Customer referral rate >15%** (customers recommend to friends/family)

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

- **Monthly Revenue:** Total sales revenue per month (target: $2,000/month by month 6)
- **Conversion Rate:** % of site visitors who make a purchase (target: 2-3%)
- **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):** Cost to acquire one customer (target: <$15)
- **Lifetime Value (LTV):** Average revenue per customer over time (target: >$100)
- **Community Impact:** Total dollars donated (target: 10-15% of net profits)
- **Order Fulfillment Time:** Time from order to shipment/pickup (target: <3 business days)

## MVP Scope

### Core Features (Must Have)

- **Product Catalog:** Display apple products with high-quality photos, descriptions, ingredients, pricing, and availability
- **Shopping Cart:** Add/remove items, update quantities, view cart total
- **Checkout Flow:** Secure payment processing (credit/debit cards), customer information collection, shipping vs. pickup selection
- **Shipping Calculator:** Real-time shipping cost calculation based on customer location and order weight
- **Local Pickup Option:** Allow customers to select pickup, with instructions and location details
- **Order Confirmation:** Email confirmation with order details and next steps
- **Basic Product Management:** Admin ability to add/edit products, manage inventory, mark items as sold out
- **Order Management:** Admin view of orders, ability to mark as fulfilled, view customer details
- **About/Mission Page:** Tell the story of 4050, heritage apples, and charitable mission
- **Mobile-Responsive Design:** Works seamlessly on phones, tablets, and desktops

### Out of Scope for MVP

- User accounts / login system
- Order tracking beyond email confirmation
- Subscription/recurring orders
- Gift cards or discount codes
- Product reviews/ratings
- Recipe content or blog
- Social media integration
- Multiple product categories beyond apples (other produce products)
- Wholesale/bulk ordering
- International shipping
- Advanced analytics dashboard

### MVP Success Criteria

The MVP is successful when:
1. A customer can browse products, place an order (shipping or pickup), and complete payment without assistance
2. Admin can manage inventory and fulfill orders efficiently
3. System generates 10+ successful transactions within first 2 weeks
4. No critical bugs prevent purchases or order fulfillment
5. Mobile experience is smooth and professional

## Technical Considerations

### Platform Requirements

- **Target Platforms:** Web-based application accessible via modern browsers
- **Browser/OS Support:** Chrome, Safari, Firefox, Edge (last 2 versions); iOS Safari, Android Chrome
- **Performance Requirements:** 
  - Page load time <3 seconds
  - Works on 3G mobile connections
  - Handles 100+ concurrent users
- **Accessibility:** WCAG 2.1 AA compliance for inclusive access

### Technology Preferences

- **Frontend:** Next.js (React framework) for modern, fast, SEO-friendly website
- **Backend:** Next.js API routes or Node.js backend for simplicity
- **Database:** PostgreSQL or MongoDB for product/order data storage
- **Hosting/Infrastructure:** Vercel (frontend), Railway/Render/Heroku (backend if needed)
- **Payment Processing:** Stripe for secure, reliable payment handling
- **Email:** SendGrid or Postmark for transactional emails

### Architecture Considerations

- **Repository Structure:** Monorepo with frontend and backend in single Next.js project
- **Service Architecture:** Start simple - serverless functions for backend logic
- **Integration Requirements:**
  - Stripe API for payments
  - Shipping rate API (USPS, UPS, or ShipStation)
  - Email service API
- **Security/Compliance:**
  - PCI compliance (handled by Stripe)
  - SSL/HTTPS for all pages
  - Secure customer data storage
  - Privacy policy and terms of service

## Constraints & Assumptions

### Constraints

- **Budget:** Bootstrap budget - minimize costs through free/low-cost tools and services
- **Timeline:** Target launch in 3-4 months to capitalize on growing season
- **Resources:** Solo operation initially - one person managing production, fulfillment, and website
- **Technical:** Must be maintainable without full-time developer (simple admin interface essential)

### Key Assumptions

- Product quality and taste will drive repeat purchases and referrals
- Customers value the social impact story enough to pay artisan pricing
- Heritage apple angle differentiates from commodity jam/jelly brands
- Local pickup will be popular with nearby customers (reduces shipping complexity)
- Email marketing and word-of-mouth will be primary customer acquisition channels
- Production capacity can scale to meet initial demand (50-100 orders/month)
- Shipping logistics can be handled efficiently without fulfillment center
- Food safety regulations and licensing are manageable (or already handled)

## Risks & Open Questions

### Key Risks

- **Production Capacity:** Risk of overwhelming demand exceeding production capability
  - *Mitigation: Start with limited inventory, clearly communicate lead times, implement waitlist*

- **Shipping Complexity:** Perishable products, temperature control, shipping costs
  - *Mitigation: Research best shipping partners, use insulated packaging, test shipments, limit shipping during extreme weather*

- **Food Safety/Licensing:** Regulatory compliance for homemade food sales
  - *Mitigation: Verify all local/state cottage food laws, obtain necessary licenses/permits before launch*

- **Customer Acquisition Cost:** Difficulty reaching target audience affordably
  - *Mitigation: Focus on organic channels (social media, local press), leverage existing gift recipients as first customers*

- **Seasonality:** Apple products may have seasonal demand patterns
  - *Mitigation: Build inventory during harvest, market as year-round/gift items, explore complementary products*

### Open Questions

- What are the specific food safety/cottage food law requirements for your location?
- What is the realistic production capacity per week/month?
- What is the target pricing for each product?
- What packaging will be used (jars, labels, shipping materials)?
- Which specific charitable organizations will be initial partners?
- How will charitable giving be communicated (% of profits, specific amounts)?
- What existing photography/content assets are available?
- Are there any legal/insurance requirements beyond food licensing?
- What is the geographic range for local pickup?
